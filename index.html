Welcome to the site!

Although people have been discovering fossils for most of human history, our knowledge of life on earth grew by leaps and bounds in the 19th century. That century saw many key fossil discoveries, from the aquatic monsters unearthed by Mary Anning in England to the many species excavated in the “Bone Wars” of the American West. The term “dinosaur” first appeared in the 19th century, as did the term “paleontology” itself. Fossil discoveries coincided with Charles Darwin's emerging theory of evolution, which provided a useful framework for understanding extinct creatures -- and also had weighty implications for humanity's place in the natural order.

This website will highlight interesting images from 19th century paleontology as a way to inspire readers to learn more about that era.

"The jolly old beast is not deceased. There's life in him again." -- Edward Forbes, 1853
