Sea monsters

One of the key figures in 19th century paleontology was Mary Anning. Anning made many spectacular fossil discoveries in the seaside cliffs of Lyme Regis, England. Some of her notable finds were bones of prehistoric aquatic reptiles such as the dolphin-like ichthyosaurs and the long-necked plesiosaurs. Anning also collected fossils of marine invertebrates, notably recovering usable ink from the remains of prehistoric squid-like creatures. Her discoveries caused a sensation, but as a working-class woman in the 19th century, she did not always receive proper credit for her work.

Trivia

Many books claim that Anning was the inspiration for the phrase "She sells seashells by the seashore." However, experts have found no clear evidence to support this claim.
