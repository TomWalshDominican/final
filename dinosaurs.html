Dinosaurs

The scientific community first recognized dinosaurs as a distinct group of animals in the 19th century. Many of the early discoveries of dinosaur fossils took place in England. In the second half of the 19th century, the United States became a hotbed of dinosaur research, as seen in the so-called “Bone Wars,” a fierce race between academic rivals to excavate fossils in the American West.
