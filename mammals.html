Mammals

The 19th century was an important era for research into prehistoric mammals. In South America, Charles Darwin himself collected several significant mammal fossils, such as giant sloth bones. Those fossil discoveries influenced Darwin's thoughts on evolution, since he could perceive similarities between extinct species and their living relatives. Later in the century, the so-called "Bone Wars" in the American West led to many important excavations of mammal fossils as academic rivals sought to describe the most new species.

Trivia

Interactive 3D models of many of the fossils discovered by Charles Darwin are available via the London Natural History Museum's website. See the Darwin's Fossil Mammals page.
